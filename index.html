Common Questions

Everything you're
about to ask.

Do I need to sign a long-term contract?

No. Month-to-month. If it's not working, you shouldn't stay. But most clients don't leave because it works.

How much do I need to spend on ads?

The management fee is separate from your ad budget. Minimum recommended spend is around $1,500/mo — less than that and Meta's algorithm doesn't have enough volume to optimize. Exact numbers depend on your market and vertical; we cover this on the strategy call.

What makes this different from every other agency?

Most agencies hand you a lead and call it a day. We handle the follow-up, the qualification, and the booking — the three places contractors bleed money. By the time a name lands on your calendar, they've already been through a full qualification process. You're not chasing anyone.

What if I don't get results?

We don't hide behind a 90-day "ramp period." If the system isn't performing, we'll tell you before you ask — and we'll fix it or part ways. Taking money for a system that isn't working isn't how this runs.

Who is this NOT for?

Contractors who can't close. This system books qualified appointments — it doesn't win jobs for you. If your problem is converting estimates you already have, fix that first and come back. Also not for anyone under $30K monthly revenue — the economics don't work at that level, and I'd rather tell you now than take your money.

How fast will I see booked appointments?

Most clients see their first booked appointments within 7–14 days of launch. Ad accounts need a few days to exit Meta's learning phase. We set realistic timelines on the strategy call — no promises we can't keep.
